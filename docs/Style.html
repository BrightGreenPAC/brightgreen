Flexibility & Extended Palette

Violet and Lime are preferred accents, with Yellow for highlights. Designers may introduce secondary/extended colors for campaigns, data visuals, or partner work—provided contrast remains accessible and new hues harmonize with the core palette.

  • Neutrals (warm/cool grays) for formal or content‑heavy layouts.
  • Alternative greens/blues for climate or policy contexts.
  • Seasonal or campaign‑specific accents with documented usage.

Typography

League Spartan H1

Roboto body text: 16–18px with 1.55–1.6 line height. Reserve ALL CAPS for short CTAs; use sentence case for longer headings.

h1–h3 { font-family: "League Spartan", sans-serif; }   body { font-family: "Roboto", sans-serif; }

Typographic Flexibility

Headlines: League Spartan is the default. Campaigns may use a compatible bold sans‑serif or display face when it improves clarity or tone; keep readability and brand harmony.

Body: Roboto is the default. Long‑form or editorial content may optionally use a highly readable serif paired with League Spartan, maintaining 1.5–1.7 line height and clear hierarchy.

Imagery & Iconography

Prefer authentic photography with natural light. Oregon backdrops are great for local identity, but abstract, illustrative, or national‑scope imagery is encouraged when it better tells the story. Use overlays to ensure warmth and legibility.

Overlay: Lime→Yellow (40%)

  • Abstract/illustrative graphics for policy explainers and data.
  • National scenes or subjects for broader campaigns.
  • Texture/shape systems as subtle brand carriers when photos aren’t available.

Motion & Interaction

  • Transitions 150–250ms, ease-in-out
  • Hover: slight brightness shift; Active: translateY(1px)
  • Cards: subtle lift on hover
  • Focus: lime ring for keyboard visibility
.btn{transition:transform .05s ease, filter .15s ease}
.btn:active{transform:translateY(1px)}

Transitions (150–250ms) and hover/active treatments are defaults. Additional subtle patterns (e.g., fades, slides, emphasis reveals) may be used for storytelling, provided they remain restrained and respect prefers‑reduced‑motion.

Accessibility & Content

  • Maintain contrast: use provided pairs (Lime/Black, Violet/White, Yellow/Black, Black/White).
  • Focus states visible: lime ring on interactive elements.
  • Minimum tap target: 44×44px; generous spacing from the scale.
  • Motion: respect prefers-reduced-motion. Avoid flashy transitions.
  • Headings are descriptive; links use meaningful text.

Copy Guidelines

  • Tone: optimistic, action‑oriented, inclusive.
  • CTAs: short, action‑oriented verbs (e.g., Donate, Volunteer, Join, Share, Sign Up, Pledge, Learn More). Match the campaign context while staying direct.
  • Use sentence case for longer headings; reserve ALL CAPS for short labels.
